The Sunday Problem

You know the drill. Every. Single. Week.

It's Sunday afternoon. Your community has a tournament on Saturday. You need to post about it on five different platforms — and every single one needs to sound completely different.

Discord wants energy. Reddit wants context. Bluesky wants wit. Instagram wants hashtags. Twitter wants brevity. And you're the one translating the same message five times, for five audiences, in five different tones.

That's before the DMs buried in spam, the coordination threads you can't find, and the stakeholder recap you still haven't written.

How it works

Your voice. Every platform. Zero soul sold.

Human First

Our belief

Community managers pour their soul into spaces other people call home. We handle the mechanics so they never have to choose between consistency and authenticity.

The Human is Irreplaceable

Every feature enhances human judgment — never bypasses it. You read the room. You know when not to post. No tool can replicate that.

Communities Deserve Authenticity

Your community built trust with you — not an AI. Suggestions should sound like you on a good day, or they're not good enough.

Relief Without Compromise

You shouldn't choose between showing up consistently and showing up fully. Hearth removes the mechanical drain without touching the magic.

Built From the Inside

Made by an ex-CM who lived every one of these problems. Not a growth hacker who read about community management once.
